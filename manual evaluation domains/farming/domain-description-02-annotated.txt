<harvest>The harvest is the production that is obtained from crops. High-quality harvests are crucial for market acceptance and consumer satisfaction.</quality> <harvested-by>Each harvest is connected to the farmer who performed the harvest and who is also responsible for evaluating the quality and quantity of the harvested crop, essential for market transactions and crop performance analysis.</harvested-by>

Various properties link machinery to specific uses, such as field preparation, planting, and harvesting.

<used-for-field-preparation>Machinery used for preparation of the field for the planting includes those machines like tractors equipped with specific attachments, used to prepare the field for planting.</used-for-field-preparation> This preparatory process might include plowing, tilling, and conditioning the soil to create an optimal planting environment. These machines are crucial as they prepare the physical space where crops will be grown, influencing everything from seed bed quality to the distribution of nutrients and water retention in the soil.

<used-for-planting>Machinery used for planting plays a pivotal role in the agricultural planting process. <used-for-harvesting>Machinery used for harvesting includes combines, harvesters, and other equipment that is primarily utilized to collect and process crops at the end of their growing cycle. Each of these types of machinery is integral to modern farming practices. They not only enhance productivity but also improve the overall efficiency of agricultural operations. By mechanizing tasks that were once labor-intensive, farms can manage larger areas more effectively, maintain higher standards of crop health and soil management, and ultimately achieve better yields.
